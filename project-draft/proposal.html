NBA Player Comparison & Prediction Tool

Problem Description

The NBA is filled with fan debates about who the best players are, yet there is no dedicated platform that allows fans to rate and rank players based on their own opinions. While sites like Basketball Reference provide extensive stats, they focus on objective data and lack a personalized or community-driven element. Fans have no easy way to express or share how they perceive players across different skill areas.

Other platforms like StatMuse or Reddit allow discussion but don’t offer structured tools for individual player ratings, community rankings, or curated favorites. Inspired by media-focused sites like IMDb and Letterboxd, this project aims to bring a more interactive, opinion-based experience to NBA fans—bridging the gap between cold stats and fan sentiment.

Proposed Application

This project introduces a web application that allows users to rate NBA players across multiple categories, including Shooting, Defense, Playmaking, and Rebounding. Each player will receive an overall community score based on fan ratings, which will be displayed on their profile alongside key stats and a highlight reel.

  • Rate players from 1 to 10 in multiple skill categories
  • View average fan scores and official stats on player pages
  • Track rating trends over time with dynamic charts
  • Create a profile to build and share a Top 10 Favorite Players list

By combining personal expression with interactive data features, the app offers a unique way for fans to engage with the NBA. It goes beyond viewing stats by allowing users to contribute their own evaluations, turning player tracking into a personalized and community-driven experience.

Target Users

Our webpage’s target audience is basketball fans, as our site is centered around the NBA. Since NBA fans come from diverse backgrounds, our site is designed to be inclusive and accessible to all. Users will interact with our version of IMDb for NBA players, where they can vote on the best players in specific categories like shooting, dunking, defense, playmaking, and rebounding. These votes will determine rankings in each category and contribute to an overall player ranking.

Each NBA player will have a profile page displaying their career averages, current stats, and user-generated rankings. Users can also customize their own profile by adding their “Top 10 favorite players” and creating custom teams using actual NBA players. These teams can then be compared with others to see which is stronger.

Interactive Features

  • Player IMDb-Style Rankings – Users rate NBA players in categories such as shooting, dunking, defense, playmaking, and rebounding. These scores contribute to both category-specific and overall player rankings.
  • Custom Team Builder – Users can create teams of 5 players and compare them against others using an algorithm to determine which is stronger.
  • NBA Player Profiles – Each player has a page showing their career and season stats, user rankings, and embedded highlight reels.
  • User Profiles – Users can create personal profiles, track players they’ve rated, and display a Top 10 Favorite Players list.

Data & References

  • NBA API – Provides real-time and historical player stats, team rosters, game schedules, results, and box scores. Useful for live updates and game-specific data needed for comparisons and ratings.
  • Basketball Reference – Offers comprehensive historical datasets, advanced metrics (PER, BPM, WS), and player career logs. Ideal for visualizations and analytics beyond real-time stats.
  • Kaggle: Complete NBA Database and Historical Box Scores – This dataset offers a comprehensive record of NBA games and player statistics from 1946 to the present day. Designed for enthusiasts, analysts, and data scientists, it provides a solid foundation for exploring basketball history, player performance, and team dynamics. • Team stats, shot charts, salary data, and draft info

Why This App?

This app stands out by combining NBA stat tracking with interactive fan participation, similar to how IMDb and Letterboxd allow users to rate and curate entertainment content. Unlike static stat websites, our tool offers polls, rankings, and dynamic insights driven by fan opinions. Features like “Matchup Insights” and custom team builders make the experience engaging and personalized, encouraging users to return, contribute, and explore the NBA in new ways.
